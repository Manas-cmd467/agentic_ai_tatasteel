1. EQUIPMENT SPECIFICATIONS — RM-01 AND RM-02
===============================================

Both rolling mills in service are 4-high reversing mills designed for hot flat rolling of steel slabs into strip and plate product.

Key Design Data:
  Mill Type                  : 4-High Reversing Hot Mill
  Work Roll Diameter         : 650 mm (new) / 590 mm (minimum before change)
  Backup Roll Diameter       : 1,350 mm
  Roll Body Length           : 2,100 mm
  Maximum Rolling Force      : 40 MN
  Drive Motor Power          : 8 MW per side (16 MW total)
  Motor Speed Range          : 0–180 rpm (spindle)
  Rolling Speed              : 0–8 m/s (entry and delivery)
  Maximum Slab Width         : 1,900 mm
  Maximum Slab Thickness     : 250 mm (entry)
  Minimum Strip Thickness    : 3.0 mm (exit, hot rolled)
  Hydraulic Gauge Control    : AGC (Automatic Gauge Control) servo-hydraulic
  Cooling Water Flow         : 500 L/min (roll body cooling per side)

3. COMMON FAULTS — DIAGNOSIS AND REPAIR PROCEDURES
====================================================

3.1 ROLL BEARING FAILURE
-------------------------
Symptoms:
  - Bearing temperature alarm (>75°C on RTD embedded in chock)
  - Vibration increase: spectral peak at rolling element frequency (BPFO/BPFI)
  - Noise increase — metallic rumble or high-frequency screeching
  - Lubricant discolouration (grey metallic particles in grease sample)
  - Roll gap scatter — bearing clearance increase causes gauge variation

Causes:
  a) Lack of lubrication — clogged grease line or long re-greasing interval
  b) Overload — rolling force exceeding bearing dynamic capacity
  c) Rolling contact fatigue — spalling after design life (typically 8,000–12,000 h)
  d) Water ingress — cooling water contaminating lubricant

Step-by-Step Bearing Replacement Procedure:
  Step 1 : Raise rolling force to zero; retract roll gap to maximum.
  Step 2 : Initiate roll change — slide work roll chock assembly to the operator-side roll-change table using the roll-changing device.
  Step 3 : Apply Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) on main drive, hydraulic power unit, and cooling water supply.
  Step 4 : Transfer chock assembly to bearing workshop using overhead crane.
  Step 5 : Heat chock to 80°C in oven; press out inner race using hydraulic press (30-tonne capacity).
  Step 6 : Clean chock bore with lint-free cloth and solvent; inspect for pitting or fretting.
  Step 7 : Install new bearing (SKF 240/630 ECAK30/W33 or equivalent); heat bearing to 100°C; press fit.
  Step 8 : Pack with approved grease (SKF LGEP 2, 500 g); install seals and seal cover.
  Step 9 : Reassemble chock on roll; verify roll neck fit (H7/k6 tolerance).
  Step 10: Return roll to mill; re-establish roll gap reference; verify temperature within 15 minutes of restart.

3.2 ROLL GAP DEVIATION (GAUGE FAILURE)
----------------------------------------
Causes:
  a) Thermal expansion of work rolls — uneven cooling water distribution
  b) Increased bearing clearance after wear
  c) Roll wear — differential wear profile across roll body
  d) AGC servo valve degradation — slow response or valve overlap change

Calibration Procedure:
  Step 1 : Stop rolling; move to pass-line position.
  Step 2 : Insert 5 mm calibration block between work rolls; record AGC position sensor reading.
  Step 3 : Compare to reference value (set during last certified calibration).
  Step 4 : If deviation > 0.08 mm: inspect hydraulic servo valve response (inject test signal to servo amplifier).
  Step 5 : Check LVDT (Linear Variable Differential Transformer) sensor: clean probe, check linearity across full stroke.
  Step 6 : After servo valve replacement or cleaning, re-calibrate at 5 mm, 20 mm, 50 mm, and 150 mm gap settings.
  Step 7 : Document calibration certificate with technician signature and date.
  Step 8 : Perform trial rolls on sacrificial slab; measure exit thickness with calibrated gauge; confirm within tolerance.

3.3 HYDRAULIC SYSTEM LEAK
---------------------------
Inspection Checklist (conduct weekly during rolling plan changeover):
  [ ] Inspect all HP hose assemblies (280 bar rated) for swelling, abrasion, or kinking
  [ ] Check AGC cylinder gland seals for weeping (wipe clean; inspect after 15 min operation)
  [ ] Inspect proportional valve manifolds for external seepage
  [ ] Check hydraulic tank level — sudden drop indicates active leak
  [ ] Sample hydraulic oil (ISO 46 HVI) — check for water contamination (>500 ppm water = replace)

Seal Replacement Procedure (AGC Cylinder):
  Step 1 : Depressurize hydraulic circuit — vent accumulator; verify zero pressure on gauge.
  Step 2 : Apply LOTO on hydraulic power unit.
  Step 3 : Remove cylinder end cap (torque 380 Nm on M42 bolts).
  Step 4 : Extract piston rod; lay on clean wooden supports.
  Step 5 : Remove worn seals (Parker Parbak + PTFE guide ring set).
  Step 6 : Clean groove with lint-free cloth; inspect for scratches (max permissible 0.3 mm depth).
  Step 7 : Fit new seals — ensure correct orientation (lip seals are directional).
  Step 8 : Lubricate seals with clean system oil before assembly.
  Step 9 : Reassemble cylinder; torque to specification; pressure test at 1.25 × working pressure.

3.4 MOTOR OVERLOAD
-------------------
Load Analysis Procedure:
  Step 1 : Retrieve rolling schedule data — identify pass sequence causing high current.
  Step 2 : Plot motor current vs. rolling force for all passes; identify outlier passes.
  Step 3 : Check roll pass schedule against material grade specification — verify reduction ratio per pass.
  Step 4 : If overload is structural (material grade mismatch): adjust pass schedule — increase pass count, reduce per-pass reduction.
  Step 5 : If overload is intermittent (slab cobble, roll bite shock): check roll cooling and billet preheat temperature.

VFD Parameter Adjustment:
  - Current limit setpoint: verify at 105% of motor rated current (do not exceed 110%)
  - Acceleration ramp: increase from 8 s to 12 s if inrush causes trips on acceleration
  - Overcurrent protection: verify relay setting is 1.15 × FLA (Full Load Amps)
  - Thermal model in VFD: verify motor parameters (resistance, inductance) match nameplate


4. PREDICTIVE MAINTENANCE SCHEDULE
=====================================

Daily Checks (Operator, each shift):
  - Record all alarm occurrences in the DCS alarm log
  - Visual inspection: look for oil on mill floor, steam, or unusual noise
  - Check cooling water manifold flow meters — compare to previous shift
  - Verify roll gap position repeats correctly after each pass reversal
  - Check hydraulic tank level — must be within ±50 mm of normal level mark

Weekly Checks (Maintenance Technician):
  - Grease all roll chock bearings (re-greasing interval: 60 operating hours)
  - Vibration reading on motor non-drive end and drive end bearings
  - Inspect all mill stand bolts — check for any loose or cracked foundations
  - Function test of all force limit trips and emergency stop circuits
  - Clean oil filter differential pressure indicators; replace if ΔP > 3 bar

Monthly Checks (Maintenance Engineer):
  - Oil sample analysis (hydraulic, gearbox, spindle)
  - Full calibration check of AGC system (as per Section 3.2)
  - Insulation resistance test on main drive motor windings (>100 MΩ @ 1,000 V)
  - Inspect roll cooling nozzles — replace clogged nozzles (flow test: each nozzle ≥ 5 L/min)
  - Ultrasonic thickness check of hydraulic hoses at wear-prone locations


5. ROLL CHANGE PROCEDURE
==========================

Required Personnel: 2 qualified roll-change technicians + 1 crane operator
Estimated Time: 45–60 minutes (work roll pair)

Step 1  : Inform Rolling Mill Operator. Stop rolling. Open roll gap to maximum.
Step 2  : Apply LOTO on both main drive motors. Verify zero energy (check with test lamp).
Step 3  : Disconnect cooling water hoses on both operator side and drive side.
Step 4  : Uncouple drive spindles — loosen spindle lock nuts (use torque multiplier).
Step 5  : Position roll-change carriage at mill stand entrance. Align rail height.
Step 6  : Push used work roll chock assembly onto carriage using the pusher cylinder.
Step 7  : Transfer used rolls to roll shop via overhead crane — attach only to certified roll lifting beams.
Step 8  : Bring pre-ground replacement rolls from roll shop (confirm ground diameter and profile on paperwork).
Step 9  : Slide new roll pair into mill stand. Engage spindles. Torque spindle lock nuts to 850 Nm.
Step 10 : Reconnect cooling water hoses. Open water supply; check for leaks.
Step 11 : Remove LOTO. Inch drive motor to verify correct rotation direction.
Step 12 : Set reference roll gap using calibration block. Document in roll change log.
Step 13 : Perform trial roll on sacrificial slab. Verify strip thickness and flatness within specification.


6. SAFETY LOCKOUT/TAGOUT (LOTO) PROCEDURE
==========================================

  LOTO Standard: IS/IEC 60204-1 compliant; mandatory for all rolling mill maintenance work.

  Step 1 : Inform all affected personnel and control room of LOTO initiation.
  Step 2 : Shut down equipment using normal stopping procedure.
  Step 3 : Identify all energy isolation points: main 11kV disconnect switch, hydraulic power unit isolator, cooling water stop valve, pneumatic supply isolation valve.
  Step 4 : Operate each isolation device to the OFF position.
  Step 5 : Apply personal padlock and danger tag to each isolation point. Each technician applies their own lock.
  Step 6 : Vent residual pressure from hydraulic accumulators (verify zero on pressure gauge).
  Step 7 : Block or restrain any gravity-hazard components (e.g., block under roll change carriage).
  Step 8 : Verify zero energy state: attempt start from operator desk — confirm no movement.
  Step 9 : Work may now proceed. Do not remove locks except by the person who applied them.
  Step 10: On work completion, reassemble equipment, remove all tools, then remove personal locks in reverse order.
