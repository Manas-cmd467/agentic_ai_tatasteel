1. EQUIPMENT OVERVIEW
======================

This guide covers two classes of rotating equipment used in the Tata Steel plant utility and process systems:

  PUMP-01 and PUMP-02  — Heavy-duty centrifugal slurry pumps (scale pit and slab descaling service)
  COMP-01 and COMP-02  — Oil-injected twin-screw air compressors (instrument air and blast air service)

Both equipment classes are monitored online by the Maintenance Wizard sensor platform and are subject to condition-based maintenance (CBM) protocols.


2. CENTRIFUGAL SLURRY PUMP SPECIFICATIONS (PUMP-01 / PUMP-02)
===============================================================

  Pump Type            : Single-stage end-suction centrifugal, back-pull-out design
  Duty                 : Slurry transport (mill scale, descaling water, sinter plant return water)
  Design Flow Rate     : 500 m³/h at rated conditions
  Design Head          : 80 m (total dynamic head)
  Fluid Specific Gravity: 1.05–1.25 (slurry)
  Solid Content (max)  : 20% by weight; particle size ≤ 25 mm
  Pump Casing Material : High chrome white iron (27% Cr)
  Impeller Material    : Hard chrome alloy (Type A05, hardness 650 HBN)
  Shaft Seal           : Mechanical seal (Type 21 — John Crane or equivalent)
  Drive Motor          : 110 kW, 6-pole, 975 rpm, IP55, TEFC
  Coupling             : Flexible disc coupling (rated 1.25 × motor torque)
  Bearing Arrangement  : Angular contact ball bearings (DE) + cylindrical roller (NDE)


3. SCREW COMPRESSOR SPECIFICATIONS (COMP-01 / COMP-02)
=======================================================

  Compressor Type       : Oil-injected twin-screw, fixed-speed with inlet modulation
  Capacity              : 1,000 Nm³/h free air delivery (at 10 bar, 20°C, 60% RH)
  Discharge Pressure    : 10 bar (g) — adjustable 7–13 bar
  Pressure Regulation   : Inlet valve modulation + blowdown valve
  Drive Motor           : 160 kW, 4-pole, 1,485 rpm, IP55, VFD-compatible
  Oil System            : Injection-cooled; integrated separator; oil volume 90 L
  Oil Specification     : Shell Corena S4 R 46 (synthetic polyol ester base)
  Cooling               : Air-cooled aftercooler + oil cooler (fin-tube, forced draught fan)
  Air Treatment Downstream: Refrigeration dryer (dewpoint +3°C), coalescing filter (0.01 micron)

5. COMMON FAULTS AND DIAGNOSIS
================================

5.1 PUMP CAVITATION
--------------------
Cavitation occurs when suction head falls below the NPSH required (NPSHr = 4.5 m for PUMP-01/02), causing vapour bubble collapse on the impeller and casing surfaces.

Symptoms:
  - Crackling, rattling noise resembling gravel in pump casing
  - Vibration spike at broadband frequencies (not synchronous)
  - Discharge pressure fluctuation (±5% or more)
  - Flow meter reading intermittent drops
  - Impeller erosion visible during inspection — pitting on suction side of vanes

Causes: Blocked suction strainer; sump level too low; suction pipe leak; excessive speed; slurry density increase beyond design.

Troubleshooting Flowchart (Pump Cavitation):
  START: Is noise/vibration type consistent with cavitation? (crackling, not tonal)
    YES --> Check sump level. Is sump level above minimum (>0.8 m above pump centreline)?
              NO  --> Increase sump level. Does noise stop? YES --> Resolved. NO --> Continue.
              YES --> Check suction strainer differential pressure. Is dP > 0.15 bar?
                        YES --> Clean strainer. Recheck symptoms.
                        NO  --> Check suction pipe for air leaks (pressurize with N2; soap test). Leak found?
                                  YES --> Repair flange gasket or valve gland. Recheck.
                                  NO  --> Check slurry SG (is it > 1.25?). YES --> Reduce feed rate. NO --> Consult engineer.
  END

5.2 MECHANICAL SEAL FAILURE
-----------------------------
Symptoms: Visible liquid drip at seal gland (>10 drops/min = failure), seal face discolouration, bearing housing becomes wet.
Causes: Loss of seal water flush, slurry ingress into seal faces, running dry during start-up.
Action: Stop pump; confirm LOTO; replace seal cartridge assembly. Inspect shaft sleeve for wear. Re-establish seal water before restart.

5.3 IMPELLER WEAR
------------------
Symptoms: Gradual flow drop (>15% below design) at rated speed and head; increased power consumption.
Cause: Abrasive wear by mill scale and silica particles.
Action: Inspect impeller clearance (front plate gap > 3 mm = worn). Replace impeller. Measure wear rate — if campaign < 1,800 h, review slurry sizing.

5.4 COMPRESSOR HIGH OIL CARRYOVER
-----------------------------------
Symptoms: Oil visible in downstream airline; compressed air sample shows oil > 5 ppm; separator differential pressure elevated.
Cause: Clogged separator element or ruptured separator element (rupture allows oil bypass).
Action: Replace separator element. Check upstream oil cooler for leaks. Verify oil level is not overfilled.

5.5 UNLOADING VALVE MALFUNCTION
---------------------------------
Symptoms: Compressor hunts between load and unload rapidly (short-cycling < 30 s); excessive power consumption.
Cause: Leaking inlet valve seat, worn pilot valve, incorrect pressure band setting.
Action: Adjust pressure band (minimum 1.5 bar differential between load and unload set-points). If hunting continues, rebuild or replace inlet valve assembly.


6. VIBRATION ANALYSIS GUIDELINES
===================================

For rotating equipment, the following frequency signatures indicate common fault types. All measurements are taken at the bearing housing, radial direction, with accelerometer sensitivity 100 mV/g.

  Fault Type                     Dominant Frequency Signature
  Unbalance (impeller/rotor)     1× Running speed (dominant radial)
  Misalignment (coupling)        2× Running speed; axial component elevated
  Bearing inner race defect      BPFI = n/2 × RPM/60 × (1 + Bd/Pd × cos α)
  Bearing outer race defect      BPFO = n/2 × RPM/60 × (1 − Bd/Pd × cos α)
  Cavitation                     Broadband noise floor elevation; no tonal peaks
  Looseness (foundation/foot)    Sub-harmonics 0.5×, 1×, 2× RPM; phase randomness

Where: n = number of rolling elements, Bd = ball diameter, Pd = pitch diameter, α = contact angle.

Trending: Collect monthly baseline readings. Alert threshold = 2× baseline or per Section 4 limits, whichever is reached first.


7. LUBRICATION SCHEDULE
=========================

Centrifugal Slurry Pumps:
  Drive-end bearing     : Re-grease every 30 days with Shell Gadus S2 V220 (NLGI 2), 30 g per grease point
  Non-drive-end bearing : Re-grease every 30 days, 20 g per point
  Coupling              : Inspect flexible element every 6 months; replace every 3 years
  Seal water            : Check flow (5 L/min) every shift; clean strainer monthly

Screw Compressors:
  Compressor oil change : Every 4,000 operating hours — use Shell Corena S4 R 46 (synthetic)
  Oil filter element    : Replace every 2,000 hours or when dP > 1.0 bar
  Air filter element    : Replace every 2,000 hours or monthly in dusty environments
  Oil separator element : Replace every 4,000 hours or when dP > 1.2 bar
  Air-end bearings      : Factory packed; inspect at 20,000 h; replace at 40,000 h or at overhaul
